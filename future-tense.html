Simple Future Tense

going to


The sentence structure:


主語 + (be) going to + 原形動詞


Is used in informal English (especially casual conversation) to talk about something that will happen in the future that has already been decided:


(A) What are you going to do on Sunday?

(B) I’m going to visit my grandparents.

will


The sentence structure:


主語 + will + 原形動詞

{ I’ll / You’ll / He’ll / She’ll / It’ll / They’ll } + 原形動詞


Can be used in formal and informal English. In informal speech “will” is often used to talk about something that will happen in the future that has just been decided:


(A) I’m cold.

(B) Me too! I’ll shut the window.

In formal English “will” can also be used to talk about things that will happen in the future that has already been decided:


The president will arrive at 4PM.
